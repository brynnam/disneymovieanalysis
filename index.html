Inclusive Ink

Exploring LGBTQ+ Visibility in Youth Cartoon Universes

Introduction

In the dynamic landscape of youth cartoons, the representation of LGBTQ characters has emerged as a topic of increasing importance and scrutiny. As society's understanding and acceptance of diverse identities evolve, so does the portrayal of LGBTQ characters in animated media. Using a detailed dataset, I hope to dive into the evolution of LGBTQ representation in youth cartoons over time, examining potential trends and patterns in their portrayal across various dimensions such as studios, platforms, and character roles. By exploring these features, I aim to answer and shed light on exactly how much progress has been made in terms of depicting LGBTQ identities within the realm of animated entertainment.
The data set I found, titled LGBTQ Characters in Youth Cartoons, is a collection of about 250 cartoon characters that have been confirmed to be a part of the LGBTQ+ community. It explores these characters in depth, spanning 13 different attributes including name, show, gender, race, role, studio, representation, orientation, year, platform, confirmation, disability (if any), and show rating. The large extent to which it examines these characters makes it the perfect to use for such an analysis as this one.

Magnitude + Longevity

First, I want to examine the presence of lgbtq+ characters by analyzing not only the number of characters present but also how important they were to each show. Examining the longevity of these characters will give a closer look and clarify if the majority of the characters representing the community are being given the opportunity to stick around in these shows.
This chart displays the total amount of characters by each studio, colored similar to a rainbow as a fun easter egg since a rainbow is very symbolic of the LGBTQ+ community as a whole. There’s also an option to view the studio character count by the role type of character, whether it be the main character (in every episode) vs. a guest star (in one episode). It’s accompanied by tooltips to help with readability.
This gives us a look into if characters are truly being represented thoroughly, or if they’re still a large minority with studios just using them to fill a quota or seem more diverse. As you can see from the chart, many studios have a large amount of LGBTQ+ characters, including the main bigger studios with the most popular kids channels (Disney, Cartoon Network, etc.). If you utilize the filtering system and compare the character count by each role, there are very few main characters, and most LGBTQ+ characters we’ve seen so far have been guest stars, or recurring characters, meaning they guest-starred in only a few characters.
